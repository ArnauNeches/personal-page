Who I am

I like to understand things fundamentally and to solve difficult problems, because of this, I chose to study Math and CS. Math is rigorous, formal and gets to the root of the things which I love, while in CS computers stop being a black box and start becoming something I can understand.

In CS my main interest is AI/ML. I am currently a research assistant at my home university at the Minerva Research Group working on attribute selection.

Appart from this, I also enjoy learning about low level stuff like computer architecture, operating systems or networks.

On the math's side, I started being an Algebra fan, then switched to analysis and topology and then back to Algebra. Now seeing it with perspective, the order I just said coincides with the branch I had the best professors each year which in conclusion tells us that I like well taught things. Okay, I accept this is very ambiguous, if it was a life or death question I would probably say analysis (Sorry Algebra fans).
